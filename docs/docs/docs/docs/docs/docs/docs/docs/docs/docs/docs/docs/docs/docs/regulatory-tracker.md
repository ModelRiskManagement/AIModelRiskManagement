# Regulatory Tracker

## Purpose of the Regulatory Tracker
A regulatory tracker provides a centralized view of global regulations, guidance, and standards related to AI, machine learning, model risk management, and large language models (LLMs). It helps organizations stay compliant, anticipate changes, and align governance practices with evolving expectations.

The tracker should be updated regularly and reviewed by risk, compliance, and legal teams.

---

# 1. United States

## SR 11-7 (Model Risk Management)
- Applies to all models, including AI and LLMs
- Requires governance, validation, monitoring, and documentation
- Foundation for U.S. model risk expectations

## OCC, FDIC, Federal Reserve Guidance
- Emphasis on fairness, consumer outcomes, and explainability
- Increasing focus on AI and ML transparency
- Expectations for vendor oversight and third‑party risk

## NIST AI RMF
- Voluntary but widely adopted
- Framework for trustworthy and responsible AI
- Aligns with governance, risk, and monitoring practices

## State-Level AI Regulations
- Emerging rules on transparency and automated decision-making
- Vary by state and industry

---

# 2. European Union

## EU AI Act
- First comprehensive AI regulation
- Risk-based framework
- Strict requirements for high-risk AI systems
- Obligations for general-purpose AI and LLMs
- Documentation, transparency, and oversight requirements

## GDPR (Relevant to AI)
- Data minimization
- Transparency
- Automated decision-making rules (Article 22)
- Data subject rights

## Digital Services Act / Digital Markets Act
- Platform accountability
- Transparency obligations
- Algorithmic risk management

---

# 3. United Kingdom

## UK AI Regulation Approach
- Principles-based, sector-led
- Focus on safety, fairness, accountability, and transparency

## FCA, PRA, Bank of England Guidance
- AI as an extension of model risk
- Expectations for explainability and governance
- Emphasis on operational resilience

---

# 4. Canada

## OSFI Guideline E-23 (Model Risk Management)
- Applies to AI and ML models
- Strong alignment with SR 11-7
- Emphasis on governance, validation, and monitoring

## AI and Data Act (AIDA)
- Proposed federal AI regulation
- Focus on high-impact AI systems
- Requirements for transparency and risk mitigation

---

# 5. Asia-Pacific

## Singapore – MAS FEAT Guidelines
- Fairness, Ethics, Accountability, Transparency
- Applies to AI in financial services
- Strong focus on explainability and fairness

## Hong Kong – HKMA Supervisory Guidance
- AI governance expectations
- Model risk management alignment
- Vendor oversight requirements

## Australia – AI Ethics Principles
- Voluntary but influential
- Emphasis on safety, fairness, and accountability

---

# 6. Global Standards and Frameworks

## ISO 42001 (AI Management Systems)
- Governance and risk management standard for AI
- Applies to traditional models, ML, and LLMs

## OECD AI Principles
- International guidelines for trustworthy AI
- Focus on transparency, fairness, and accountability

## G7 and G20 AI Frameworks
- High-level principles for safe and responsible AI

---

# 7. Tracking and Updating Process

## Update Frequency
- Monthly for emerging regulations
- Quarterly for formal reviews
- Immediately for major regulatory releases

## Responsible Teams
- Compliance
- Legal
- Model Risk Management
- AI Governance

## Required Artifacts
- Summary of regulatory changes
- Impact assessments
- Required control updates
- Implementation timelines

---

# Summary
The regulatory landscape for AI, ML, and LLMs is rapidly evolving across jurisdictions. A regulatory tracker ensures organizations stay aligned with global expectations, maintain compliance, and proactively adjust governance frameworks. Regular updates and cross-functional collaboration are essential for effective regulatory readiness.
