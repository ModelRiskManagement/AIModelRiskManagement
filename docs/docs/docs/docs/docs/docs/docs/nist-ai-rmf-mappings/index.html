    NIST AI RMF Mappings¶

    Overview of the NIST AI RMF¶

    The NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF) provides a structured approach for managing risks associated with artificial intelligence systems. It is designed to help organizations develop, deploy, and use AI responsibly by focusing on trustworthiness, safety, and accountability.

    The framework is voluntary but widely adopted as a best-practice standard across industries.

    Core Functions of the NIST AI RMF¶

    The AI RMF is organized into four core functions:

    Govern¶

    Establishes organizational policies, structures, and processes to manage AI risks.

    Map¶

    Identifies and contextualizes AI risks, use cases, and system characteristics.

    Measure¶

    Assesses, analyzes, and tests AI systems to understand risk levels and performance.

    Manage¶

    Implements risk controls, monitoring, and continuous improvement.

    These functions align closely with model risk management and AI governance practices.

    Mapping to Model Risk Management¶

    The NIST AI RMF maps naturally to traditional model risk management (MRM) frameworks:

    Govern → Governance and Oversight¶

    • Roles and responsibilities
    • Policies and standards
    • Risk appetite and escalation paths

    Map → Model Inventory and Risk Tiering¶

    • Identifying model purpose and use case
    • Understanding data, assumptions, and limitations
    • Classifying model risk levels

    Measure → Validation and Testing¶

    • Performance evaluation
    • Bias and fairness testing
    • Robustness and stability analysis
    • Explainability assessments

    Manage → Monitoring and Change Management¶

    • Drift detection
    • Incident response
    • Retraining and updates
    • Continuous improvement

    The AI RMF reinforces the need for strong governance across the entire model lifecycle.

    Mapping to AI and ML Governance¶

    For AI and ML systems, the AI RMF provides additional guidance:

    Govern¶

    • Ethical principles
    • Transparency expectations
    • Human oversight requirements

    Map¶

    • Understanding training data and model architecture
    • Identifying potential harms and failure modes
    • Assessing bias and fairness risks

    Measure¶

    • Stress testing and adversarial testing
    • Explainability and interpretability evaluations
    • Bias and fairness metrics

    Manage¶

    • Monitoring for drift and degradation
    • Updating models responsibly
    • Documenting decisions and controls

    The AI RMF supports responsible AI development and deployment.

    Mapping to LLM Governance¶

    LLMs introduce unique risks that align with the AI RMF functions:

    Govern¶

    • Usage policies for generative AI
    • Guardrail and safety requirements
    • Human-in-the-loop expectations

    Map¶

    • Identifying hallucination risks
    • Understanding prompt sensitivity
    • Assessing data leakage and privacy concerns

    Measure¶

    • Hallucination testing
    • Toxicity and bias evaluations
    • Prompt evaluation and consistency checks

    Manage¶

    • Monitoring for unsafe or inaccurate outputs
    • Updating prompts, guardrails, and retrieval systems
    • Escalation and remediation procedures

    The AI RMF provides a strong foundation for governing LLMs responsibly.

    Implementation Considerations¶

    Organizations implementing the AI RMF should:

    • Integrate it with existing MRM frameworks
    • Align with regulatory requirements (e.g., EU AI Act, SR 11-7)
    • Establish cross-functional governance committees
    • Build documentation and audit trails
    • Implement continuous monitoring and improvement processes

    The AI RMF is most effective when embedded into existing governance structures.

    Summary¶

    The NIST AI RMF provides a comprehensive, flexible framework for managing AI risks. It aligns closely with model risk management, AI governance, and LLM oversight practices. By mapping the AI RMF to existing frameworks, organizations can strengthen their governance, improve transparency, and ensure responsible AI deployment.
