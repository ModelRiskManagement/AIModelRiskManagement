# EU AI Act: Model Governance Requirements 

## Overview of the EU AI Act
The EU AI Act is a comprehensive regulatory framework designed to ensure that artificial intelligence systems placed on the EU market are safe, transparent, and respect fundamental rights. It introduces risk-based obligations for AI systems, with the strictest requirements applied to high-risk AI.

For organizations using AI, ML, and LLMs, the EU AI Act has direct implications for governance, documentation, monitoring, and accountability.

## Risk Categories and Obligations
The EU AI Act classifies AI systems into several risk categories:

### Unacceptable Risk
- AI systems that pose a clear threat to safety, livelihoods, or rights
- Prohibited systems (e.g., certain types of social scoring)

### High Risk
- AI systems used in critical areas such as:
  - Creditworthiness and financial services
  - Employment and worker management
  - Education and exams
  - Law enforcement and border control
  - Critical infrastructure

High-risk systems are subject to strict governance, documentation, and oversight requirements.

### Limited and Minimal Risk
- AI systems with lower impact
- Subject to transparency obligations and good practices, but fewer formal requirements

Organizations must identify whether their AI systems fall into high-risk categories and apply appropriate controls.

## Requirements for High-Risk AI Systems
High-risk AI systems must comply with a set of core requirements, including:

- Risk management system
- High-quality and relevant training, validation, and testing data
- Technical documentation
- Record-keeping and logging
- Transparency and user information
- Human oversight
- Robustness, accuracy, and cybersecurity

These requirements align closely with model risk management and AI governance practices.

## Governance Expectations for LLMs
General-purpose AI models and LLMs may fall under specific provisions of the EU AI Act, especially when:

- They are integrated into high-risk systems
- They are deployed at scale
- They pose systemic risks

Governance expectations for LLMs include:

- Clear documentation of capabilities and limitations
- Transparency about training data sources (at an appropriate level)
- Risk mitigation measures for misuse, bias, and harmful outputs
- Technical and organizational controls for safety and alignment

Organizations using LLMs in high-impact contexts must treat them as part of a regulated AI system.

## Documentation and Transparency Requirements
The EU AI Act places strong emphasis on documentation and transparency, including:

- Detailed technical documentation
- Model purpose and intended use
- Design and development process
- Data sources and data governance
- Performance metrics and limitations
- Risk management measures
- Human oversight mechanisms

Documentation must be sufficient for regulators and notified bodies to assess compliance.

## Conformity Assessments
High-risk AI systems are subject to conformity assessments before being placed on the EU market or put into service. This may include:

- Internal control checks
- Third-party assessments (for certain use cases)
- Ongoing post-market monitoring

Organizations must be able to demonstrate that their AI systems meet the requirements of the EU AI Act through evidence and documentation.

## Enforcement and Penalties
Non-compliance with the EU AI Act can result in:

- Significant administrative fines
- Restrictions on placing AI systems on the market
- Reputational damage
- Increased regulatory scrutiny

Penalties are calibrated based on the severity and nature of the violation, with higher fines for breaches related to prohibited practices or high-risk systems.

## Summary
The EU AI Act establishes a risk-based framework for governing AI systems, with strict requirements for high-risk applications. For organizations using AI, ML, and LLMs, this means stronger expectations around governance, documentation, monitoring, and human oversight. Aligning model risk management and AI governance practices with the EU AI Act is essential for operating responsibly in EU markets and building trust with regulators, customers, and partners.
